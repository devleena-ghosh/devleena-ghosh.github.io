About

I am currently a Visiting Scientist in the Electronics and Communication Sciences Unit (ECSU), Indian Statistical Institute, Kolkata. I have recently completed my PhD the Department of Computer Science and Engineering, IIT Kharagpur in the area of development of computational methods for modelling and analysis of biological pathways specially thyrotropic regulation pathway under the supervision of Prof. Chittaranjan Mandal. The objective of this work is to develop mathematical model to capture thyroid physiology and to develop parameter estimation technique when time-series data is rarely available. Parameter identifiability analysis is also explored. An important aspect of this work is application formal methods in conjuction of machine learning approaches. Prior to this, I worked as a software engineer at Microsoft India as a member of the Visual Studio Online Services team for few years. I completed my M.Tech in Computer Science and Engineering from Indian Institute of Technology, Kharagpur (summa cum laude) where my master's thesis was on application of formal methods in railway interlocking systems. I did B.E. in Computer Science and Technology from Bengal Engineering and Science University, Shibpur (now Indian Institute of Engineering Science and Technology, Shibpur). After completing B.E., I worked as a junior research fellow in the virtual laboratory project where we have developed a virtual laboratory on logic design which is also being used as part of curriculum at IIT Kharagpur.
